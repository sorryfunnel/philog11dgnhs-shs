Transition to Day 2

The earliest Western philosophers began by trying to answer Metaphysical questions about the fundamental structure of the world. But how did they move from religious belief to rational inquiry?

The Greek Intellectual Shift: From Mythos to Logos

Mythos (Myth)

Definition: Explanations based on tradition, gods, and religious or cultural stories.

Example: "The sun is pulled across the sky every morning by the god Helios's chariot." (Explanation by Divine Will)

Logos (Reason)

Definition: Explanations based on rational inquiry, evidence, logic, and natural causes.

Example: "The sun appears to rise because the Earth is revolving on its axis and orbiting the sun." (Explanation by Rational Observation)

Thales and the Arche

Thales of Miletus was one of the first to propose a Logos-based answer to the great metaphysical question: What is the Arche (fundamental substance) of all things?

His answer was Water, not because a god commanded it, but because he observed that water is essential to life, can change forms (solid, liquid, gas), and seems to be everywhere. This shift from divine decree to rational argument marks the birth of Western philosophy.
