From Wisdom to Existence: Why Are We Here?

The Existential Question

"Why are we existing in this world?"

(Teacher facilitates student answers and notes)

The Philosophical Response (Existentialism)

This fundamental question bridges the gap between philosophy as a 'love of wisdom' and philosophy as a discipline. The philosophical answer is that existence precedes essence: we are born free, and therefore, our ultimate meaning must be created through our freedom and the responsibility we take for our actions. This is about 'putting meaning to what is out there.'

The Analytical Branches: Logic, Knowledge, and Reality

These core branches focus on the structure of thought and the nature of things.

Logic (The Science of Correct Thinking)

Focuses on valid argument and objective, reliable information.

  • Types: Deductive (General → Specific) and Inductive (Specific → General).
  • Flawed Reasoning (Fallacies): Discuss Hasty Generalization and Ad Hominem.

Epistemology (The Study of Knowledge)

Focuses on the sources and criteria of what we call knowledge.

  • Rationalism: Knowledge primarily comes from Reason (e.g., Mathematical proofs).
  • Empiricism: Knowledge primarily comes from Sense-Perception/Experience (e.g., Learning to ride a bike).

Metaphysics (The Nature of Ultimate Reality)

Focuses on existence, reality, mind-body, and time.

Case Discussion: The Filipino concept of the Mañana Habit. Is this a reflection of an underlying cultural reality, a psychological response to time, or simply a form of social laziness?

Applied Branches: Beauty and Society

How do philosophy's branches shape our value judgments about art and community?

Aesthetics (The Study of Beauty)

Case Discussion: The Korean Standard of Beauty. Is beauty universal (a Metaphysical property) or is it culturally constructed and relative (Aesthetics)? How do media and philosophical assumptions shape what we perceive as 'good looks'?

Political Philosophy (The Study of Society)

Case Discussion: The PUVMP (Public Utility Vehicle Modernization Program). Analyze the argument: Is the government pushing it primarily for the safety of riders, or is the revitalization of the sluggish Philippine economy the ultimate political goal? Which objective reflects a better political philosophy?

Making the Abstraction

Philosophy is a comprehensive discipline. When faced with a question, a philosopher knows where to categorize it: Is it about valid argument (Logic)? About what is true (Epistemology)? About ultimate reality (Metaphysics)? About society (Political Philosophy)? Or about what is beautiful (Aesthetics)?
