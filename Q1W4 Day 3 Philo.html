Comparison: The Tale of Two Truths

How do these two types of truth differ? Let's break it down by Source, Methodology, and Certainty.

CRITERIA 1 SOURCE (Where does it come from?)

Scientific Truth

Comes from Empirical Observation (The Senses). We see, touch, hear, or measure it.

Philosophical Truth

Comes from Reason & Logic (The Mind). We analyze concepts and arguments.

CRITERIA 2 METHODOLOGY (How do we find it?)

Scientific Method

Experimentation, gathering quantitative data, testing hypotheses, and repetition.

Dialectics & Logic

Socratic questioning, thought experiments (e.g., The Trolley Problem), and deductive reasoning.

CRITERIA 3 CERTAINTY (Is it final?)

Provisional

High certainty, but open to change if new data arrives (e.g., Newtonian Physics vs. Einstein). A fact, until proven otherwise.

Contextual / Logical

Depends on the strength of the premises. Logical truths are absolute; Ethical/Metaphysical truths are often debated.

Day 3 Takeaway

Science

Gives us facts, mechanics, and data about the physical universe.

Philosophy

Gives us meaning, ethics, and understanding of concepts beyond the physical.
