Key Eastern Traditions: India

Indian Philosophy: Breaking the Cycle

The central goal of many Indian traditions (like Hinduism and Buddhism) is liberation from the cycle of suffering and rebirth (Samsara).

  • Karma & Dharma:

    • Your Karma (actions) determine your destiny. Your Dharma is your ethical duty.

  • Buddhism: The Four Noble Truths

    • Suffering comes from attachment; letting go leads to peace.

Vignette: The Frustrated Musician

"Kenji practices guitar for hours but gets angry when he makes mistakes. He realizes his suffering comes from his attachment to being perfect. When he lets go of the need for perfection and just focuses on playing, his anger vanishes."

Key Eastern Traditions: China - Confucianism

Confucianism: Social Harmony

Confucianism emphasizes collective harmony through proper relationships and Filial Piety (Xiao).

  • The Five Relationships:

    • Ruler-Subject, Father-Son, Husband-Wife, Elder-Younger Sibling, Friend-Friend.

Vignette: The Family Decision

"Mei is a successful CEO, but when her family meets to discuss their small ancestral farm, she defers to her older brother's final decision. Even though she is wealthier, she respects the Elder Brother-Younger Sister relationship to maintain family harmony."

Key Eastern Traditions: China - Taoism

Taoism: The Way of Nature

Taoism focuses on living in alignment with the Tao (The Way). The core concept is Wu Wei (Non-Action/Effortless Action).

Vignette: The Traffic Jam

Wrap-up & Day 4 Preparation

Connection Question

Which philosophical branch (from Day 1) would be most concerned with a principle like Wu Wei?

The 5 Major Branches of Philosophy (Reference):

  • Metaphysics: Deals with the nature of reality and existence.
  • Epistemology: Deals with the nature of knowledge, belief, and truth.
  • Ethics: Deals with moral values, right and wrong conduct.
  • Aesthetics: Deals with the nature of art, beauty, and taste.
  • Logic: Deals with the principles of correct reasoning.
Answer: Metaphysics (Reality, because the Tao is the fundamental reality) or Ethics (Conduct, because Wu Wei dictates proper action).
